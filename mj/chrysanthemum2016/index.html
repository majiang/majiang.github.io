6th Chrysanthemum Cup: the yearly two-day open tournament of JMSA Atsugi

Abstract

Japan Mahjong Sports Association (JMSA) Atsugi branch will host its 6th two-day open torunament Chrysanthemum Cup (Cho-yo hai) in September 2016. It is a competition of pairs.

Date and time

September 17 and 18, 2016: Start on Saturday afternoon and finish in Sunday evening.

Venue

Atsugi Kenko Mahjong Center Yotsuba-kai: Two-minute walk from Hon-atsugi station.

Fee

JPY 10,000 (including the dinner of the first day and the lunch for the second day).

Rules

Official MCR rules of Japan Mahjong Sports Association.

Registration

For players from abroad, contact me (Ryuichi OHORI) via Facebook or by email for registration or inquiry. I strongly recommend contacting me as soon as you're interested in this tournament, even if you haven't decided to participate. The maximum number of players is 56.

Rules

The official MCR rules of JMSA:

  • No flower tiles
  • Seven pairs and All types cannot be combined
  • Concealed-only fans cannot be combined with Fully concealed, but can be combined with Self-drawn
  • The maximum points of a deal is limited to 88(+8)
  • Seat change only between South (2nd) and West (3rd) rounds
  • Use automatic mahjong tables, throw dice only once

Structure

  • Mainly pairs, and individual competition
  • The score of a pair is the sum of two players' table points [4, 2, 1, 0].
  • The score of a individual player is the sum of the mini-points.
  • The table assignment of Session 6 is determined according to individual ranking, but without playing against your partner

Prizes

Prizes are for (this is the last year's: this is subject to change without notification):

  • Top 3 pairs
  • Middle-ranked pair
  • Top 3 individuals
  • Lucky 7th individual
  • Hassaku (a Japanese citrus hybrid) (8(HAchi)th and 39(SAn-ju-KU)th) individuals
  • Middle-ranked individual
  • 5, 10, 15, ... th individuals
  • O-nami (Big wave): the pair with biggest individual score difference
  • Ko-nami (small wave): the pair with smallest individual score difference
  • Booby pair (in Japan, it means second from the last place)
  • Session best scores
  • Session worst scores
