The Mean Square Quasi-Monte Carlo Error for Digitally Shifted Digital Nets

This page lists the point set files we used in the talks in MCQMC2014 and paper (under review).

The format of these files is described in another page, and as a CSV each file additionally has nine columns. Values of these columns correspond to our criterion and the root mean square errors of eight test functions, in the same order as the paper.

Randomly generated digital nets

  • s = 4, m = 10
  • s = 4, m = 12
  • s = 12, m = 10
  • s = 12, m = 12

Good digital nets

  • s = 4, 8 ≤ m ≤ 20
  • s = 12, 8 ≤ m ≤ 20

Niederreiter-Xing sequence (for comparison with good digital nets above)

These files are made by converting from Magic point shop by Dirk Nuyens.

  • s = 4, 8 ≤ m ≤ 20 from nxmats/nx_b2_m30_s4_Cs
  • s = 12, 8 ≤ m ≤ 20 from nxmats/nx_b2_m30_s12_Cs
